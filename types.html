Types of Yoga

Vinyasa


A Vinyasa yoga class is when poses flow from one to the next without the teacher stopping to explain the finer points of each pose. This means that you come away with a good workout as well as a yoga experience.

Bikram


The Bikram yoga class entails a total of 26 yoga poses that are designed to stretch and strengthen the muscles as well as compress and rinse the organs of the body. The poses are usually done in a heated room to facilitate the release of toxins.

Kundalini


Kundalini yoga practice is equal parts spiritual and physical. This style is all about releasing the kundalini energy in your body said to be trapped, or coiled, in the lower spine.

Yin


Yin yoga focuses on passive, seated postures that target the tissues in the hips, pelvis and lower spine. It's ideal for atheltic types who need to release tension in joints or to just relax.

Hot


Hot yoga usually takes place in a room heated up to 38 degrees. This helps the body to prepare for movement and to remove impurities. This helps the body to de-stress, giving you an intense workout and improves your strength and flexibility.

Chair


Chair yoga is an adapted form of yoga for those who may have difficulty standing or moving easily from a standing to seated pose. It's great for those who need more support, those managing an injury or who want a more theraputic approach.
